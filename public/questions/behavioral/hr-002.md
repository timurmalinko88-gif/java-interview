# 2-minute Self-Presentation (Elevator Pitch)

## Framework
A strong elevator pitch should briefly cover:
1.  **Who you are:** Your current role and years of experience.
2.  **What you do best:** Your core technical strengths (e.g., Java, Spring Boot, system architecture).
3.  **Your recent achievements:** One or two major impacts you've made.
4.  **Why you're here:** What you are looking for in your next role and why this company is a fit.

## Real-world Java Engineering Scenarios

### Scenario 1: The Backend Specialist
"I'm a Senior Backend Engineer with 6 years of experience, specializing in Java and Spring Boot. At my current company, I designed and implemented a microservices architecture that scaled to handle 10,000 requests per second. I'm particularly proud of optimizing our Kafka message processing, reducing latency by 40%. I'm looking to bring my expertise in high-load distributed systems to your team, as I admire your engineering culture."

### Scenario 2: The Full-Stack Problem Solver
"I'm a Full-Stack Developer with a strong focus on the Java ecosystem. Over the past 4 years, I've built end-to-end features using Angular and Java 17. Recently, I led a project to migrate our legacy monolith to a containerized cloud-native application, saving 30% in infrastructure costs. I'm excited about this opportunity because it allows me to focus more on complex backend algorithms, which is where my true passion lies."

### Scenario 3: The Tech Lead
"I'm a Tech Lead with 8 years of Java experience. My sweet spot is bridging the gap between business requirements and technical execution. Recently, I led a team of 5 engineers to deliver a new payment gateway integration two weeks ahead of schedule by implementing rigorous TDD practices and CI/CD pipelines. I'm looking for a role where I can continue mentoring developers while architecting scalable Java solutions."

## Red Flags (What NOT to say)
*   **Reciting your resume:** The interviewer already read it. Highlight key themes, don't read bullet points.
*   **Being too long-winded:** Exceeding 2 minutes. Keep it punchy and engaging.
*   **Starting from childhood:** "I got my first computer when I was 10..." Start with your professional career.
*   **Negativity:** Sounding desperate or bad-mouthing your current situation. Keep the tone forward-looking and positive.
