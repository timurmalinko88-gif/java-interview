# English Tech Interview glossary & phrases
Common English phrases, idioms, and vocabulary used in international technical interviews.

---ANSWER---

When interviewing in English (especially if it's your second language), using the right professional vocabulary and idioms can make you sound much more fluent and culturally aligned with Western companies. Avoid direct translations from your native language; instead, use standard industry phrases.

### Key Vocabulary & Idioms
1. **Taking Ownership & Responsibility**:
   - *Instead of:* "I did the task."
   - *Say:* "I **took ownership** of the module." / "I **spearheaded** the migration." / "I was **accountable** for the delivery."
2. **Problem Solving & Action**:
   - *Instead of:* "We had a big problem."
   - *Say:* "We faced a significant **bottleneck**." / "There was a critical **showstopper** bug."
   - *Instead of:* "I fixed it."
   - *Say:* "I **resolved the issue** by..." / "I implemented a **workaround**." / "I **troubleshot** the system."
3. **Collaboration & Communication**:
   - *Instead of:* "I talked to the manager."
   - *Say:* "I **synced up** with the Product Owner." / "We got on the **same page**." / "I **kept stakeholders in the loop**."
   - *Instead of:* "I disagreed."
   - *Say:* "I **pushed back** on the requirements because..." / "We had to **compromise** on the scope."

### Useful Sentence Starters for the STAR Method
- **Situation**: "To give you some context..." / "In my previous role at X, we were facing a challenge with..."
- **Task**: "My main responsibility was to..." / "I was tasked with finding a solution for..."
- **Action**: "To tackle this, I first..." / "My approach was to..." / "I took the initiative to..."
- **Result**: "As a result, we managed to..." / "The outcome was highly positive; we decreased latency by..." / "Looking back, the biggest takeaway was..."

### Red Flags (Life Analogy / Anti-patterns)
- **Using slang or overly casual language**: "I was like, no way, this code is trash." Use professional terminology ("The legacy codebase had significant technical debt").
- **Apologizing too much for your English**: If you make a grammar mistake, just keep going. Confidence matters more than perfect grammar in a tech interview.
- **Directly translating native idioms**: This often causes confusion. Stick to standard business English.

### Key Points
- Practice your STAR stories out loud in English to build muscle memory.
- Use strong action verbs (spearheaded, designed, optimized, resolved).
- Focus on clarity and concise delivery over using overly complex vocabulary.
