# English Tech Interview glossary & phrases

## Framework
Using the right vocabulary demonstrates not just English proficiency, but also cultural immersion in the global software engineering community. Avoid translating idioms literally from your native language; instead, use standard industry phrasing.

## Key Glossary

*   **Tech Debt:** The implied cost of additional rework caused by choosing an easy (limited) solution now instead of using a better approach that would take longer.
*   **MVP (Minimum Viable Product):** A version of a product with just enough features to be usable by early customers who can then provide feedback for future product development.
*   **Post-mortem:** A process, usually performed at the conclusion of a project or after a major incident (outage), to determine and analyze elements of the process that were successful or unsuccessful. (Often called "blameless post-mortem").
*   **Bandwidth:** A person's capacity or available time to take on new tasks (e.g., "I don't have the bandwidth for this right now.")
*   **To deprecate:** To mark a feature or API as obsolete and discourage its use, usually warning that it will be removed in the future.

## Useful Phrases for Interviews

### Describing Experience
*   "I **spearheaded** the migration to microservices..." (Led/initiated)
*   "I have a **proven track record** of delivering scalable systems..." (History of success)
*   "My **core competency** lies in backend optimization..." (Main strength)
*   "I was heavily involved in **architecting from scratch**..." (Building from zero)

### Handling Difficult Questions
*   "That's a great question. Let me **think out loud** for a moment..." (Buying time gracefully)
*   "I haven't encountered that specific issue, but if I did, my **approach would be**..." (Showing problem-solving skills when lacking direct experience)
*   "To give you some **context**..." (Providing background information before jumping into the STAR method)

### Discussing Teamwork
*   "I **collaborated cross-functionally** with the product and design teams."
*   "We practice **pair programming** to ensure code quality and knowledge sharing."
*   "I **mentored** junior developers to help them ramp up on the project."

## Red Flags (What NOT to say)
*   **"I am normal developer."** Use standard titles like "Software Engineer" or "Backend Developer."
*   **"I make codes."** Say "I write code" or "I develop software."
*   **"We did..." (when you mean "I did").** Be precise about your personal contribution. Use "I" for your tasks, "We" for team tasks.
*   **Overusing buzzwords without understanding them.** Don't say "I leveraged AI on the blockchain to synergize the cloud" unless you can explain exactly how.
