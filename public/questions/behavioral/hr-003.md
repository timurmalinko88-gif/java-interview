# Why are you leaving your current job?

## Framework
Use the "Push-Pull" strategy. Briefly mention a neutral "push" (why you are moving on) and heavily emphasize the "pull" (why you are excited about the new opportunity).

*   **Push (Brief & Positive):** State a valid, non-toxic reason for leaving (e.g., seeking growth, company pivot, end of a long project).
*   **Pull (Detailed & Enthusiastic):** Explain what attracts you to the prospective company (tech stack, scale, domain, culture).

## Real-world Java Engineering Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Seeking Modern Tech Stack
*   **Situation/Task:** "I’ve learned a lot over the last 3 years maintaining our core billing system. However, the stack is mostly Java 8 and legacy frameworks, and there are no plans to upgrade."
*   **Action/Result:** "I have been upskilling in Java 21, Spring Boot 3, and Kubernetes on my own time. I'm looking for a role where I can apply these modern cloud-native technologies in production, which is exactly what your engineering team is doing."

### Scenario 2: Craving Scale and Complexity
*   **Situation/Task:** "I've successfully helped build our startup's MVP, and we've reached a stable product-market fit."
*   **Action/Result:** "Now that the pace has shifted to minor feature iterations, I find myself wanting to tackle harder architectural challenges related to high throughput and distributed systems. Your company's scale of millions of users offers exactly the complex Java engineering challenges I'm looking for."

### Scenario 3: Looking for Mentorship/Leadership Opportunities
*   **Situation/Task:** "I've become the most senior developer on my current team, which has been great for autonomy, but I'm hitting a ceiling in terms of technical mentorship."
*   **Action/Result:** "I'm looking to join a larger, more established engineering organization where I can both learn from seasoned architects and have the opportunity to officially mentor junior developers."

## Red Flags (What NOT to say)
*   **"My boss is terrible."** Never badmouth current or former employers. It makes you look difficult to work with.
*   **"I want more money."** Even if it's true, frame it around career growth and responsibilities.
*   **"I'm bored."** This implies a lack of initiative. Say you are seeking "new challenges" instead.
*   **"I hate the code."** Complaining about legacy code shows a lack of pragmatism.
