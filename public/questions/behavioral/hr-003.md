# Why are you leaving your current job?
"Why are you looking for a new opportunity?" / "Why do you want to leave your current company?"

---ANSWER---

The key to answering this question is to remain **positive and forward-looking**. You should focus on what you want to *move towards* (growth, new challenges, specific technologies) rather than what you are trying to *escape from* (bad boss, toxic culture, legacy code). 

Even if your current situation is terrible, framing it positively shows professionalism, maturity, and emotional intelligence.

### Examples
1. **Seeking Technical Growth (Java Engineer)**: "I've learned a lot at my current company over the last three years, especially in maintaining robust Enterprise applications. However, our tech stack is quite traditional (Java 8, on-premise servers). I'm eager to transition into a role where I can work with modern cloud infrastructure, microservices, and newer Java versions, which is why your engineering culture really appeals to me."
2. **Seeking Product Impact**: "I've really enjoyed my time at my current agency, where I've worked on many different short-term client projects. But I've realized I want to take more long-term ownership of a product. I'm looking for a product company where I can see the long-term impact of my code, iterate on user feedback, and dive deeper into domain-driven design."
3. **Seeking Scale and Challenge**: "My current team is great, and we've successfully launched our MVP. However, the user base is relatively small. I'm looking for an opportunity to work on high-load systems where performance, concurrency, and scalability are critical challenges on a daily basis. Your system handling millions of requests per day is exactly the kind of environment I want to grow in."

### Red Flags (Life Analogy / Anti-patterns)
- **Badmouthing your employer**: "My manager is incompetent" or "The company is going bankrupt." Even if true, it makes you look negative and difficult to work with.
- **Focusing only on money**: "I want a higher salary." While true for most people, it shouldn't be the *primary* reason you give in an interview. Focus on value and growth first.
- **Complaining about the code**: "The codebase is a mess of legacy code." Every company has tech debt. Complaining about it suggests you might not be willing to help fix it.

### Key Points
- Always frame your departure positively. Focus on the "pull" factors of the new job, not the "push" factors of the old one.
- Align your reasons with the opportunities the new company provides.
- Keep the answer relatively brief and pivot to why you are excited about the role you are interviewing for.
