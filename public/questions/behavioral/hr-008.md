# Salary Negotiation strategies

## Framework
Negotiation is about leverage, market research, and clear communication. The goal is to reach a mutually beneficial agreement without sounding combative or arrogant.

*   **Rule 1: Delay the number.** Try to avoid giving the first number. Focus on fit and value first.
*   **Rule 2: Do your research.** Know the market rate for a Java Engineer with your experience in that specific city/remote market.
*   **Rule 3: Anchor high (if you must).** If forced to give a number, give a range where your ideal salary is at the bottom of the range.
*   **Rule 4: Negotiate total compensation.** Salary is just one part. Consider equity, bonuses, PTO, and remote work stipends.

## Actionable Scenarios & Scripts

### Scenario 1: The Initial Recruiter Call
*   **Situation:** The recruiter asks, "What are your salary expectations?" during the very first screening call.
*   **Action (Script):** "Right now, my priority is finding the right fit in terms of team, tech stack, and engineering culture. I’m confident that if we decide to move forward, we can agree on a compensation package that aligns with the market rate for my experience level. Could you share the budget approved for this role?"
*   **Result:** You deflect giving a number, show you are focused on the role, and often get the recruiter to reveal their budget.

### Scenario 2: The Lowball Offer
*   **Situation:** You receive an offer that is 15% below your target salary.
*   **Action (Script):** "Thank you so much for the offer, I'm very excited about the prospect of joining the team. However, based on my research and the current market rates for Senior Java Developers with cloud architecture experience in this area, I was expecting something closer to $X. Is there flexibility to bring the base salary up to that level?"
*   **Result:** You frame the counter-offer objectively based on market data, not personal need, maintaining a professional tone.

### Scenario 3: Negotiating Beyond Base Salary
*   **Situation:** The company says, "We literally cannot go higher on the base salary, $X is the absolute maximum for this band."
*   **Action (Script):** "I understand the constraints on the base salary. Since we are close, is there flexibility to bridge the gap with a sign-on bonus, an increase in stock options, or an extra week of paid time off?"
*   **Result:** You show flexibility and find alternative ways to increase your total compensation package.

## Red Flags (What NOT to say)
*   **"I need $X because of my mortgage/expenses."** Your personal expenses are irrelevant to your market value.
*   **"I make $X right now."** Never disclose your current salary. It caps your negotiating power. (In many places, it's illegal for them to even ask).
*   **Accepting immediately:** Always take at least 24 hours to review an offer, even if it's great. It shows prudence and gives you time to think.
*   **Ultimatums:** "Give me $X or I walk." This creates an adversarial relationship before you even start working there.
