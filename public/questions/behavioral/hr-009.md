# Questions the candidate MUST ask the interviewer

## Framework
Asking intelligent questions at the end of the interview demonstrates your engagement, seniority, and cultural fit. It also helps you evaluate if the company is a toxic environment or a great place to work.

Divide your questions based on who you are talking to:
1.  **HR/Recruiters:** Focus on culture, process, and company health.
2.  **Engineering Managers:** Focus on team structure, growth, and delivery processes.
3.  **Engineers/Tech Leads:** Focus on tech stack, daily routine, and technical debt.

## Highly Actionable Questions

### For HR / Recruiters
*   **"How has the company culture evolved over the past year?"** (Look for specific examples, not just buzzwords like "we are a family".)
*   **"What is the company's approach to remote work and work-life balance?"** (Are there core hours? Do people work weekends?)
*   **"Why is this position open?"** (Is it a new role due to growth, or did someone leave? If they left, why?)

### For Engineering Managers
*   **"How are technical decisions made within the team?"** (Is it top-down dictation, or collaborative design docs/RFCs?)
*   **"How do you balance new feature development with managing technical debt?"** (Look for a structured approach, e.g., "We dedicate 20% of every sprint to tech debt.")
*   **"What does the onboarding process look like for the first 30, 60, and 90 days?"** (A good team has a clear plan for your success.)

### For Engineers / Tech Leads
*   **"Can you walk me through the journey of a piece of code from my laptop to production?"** (This reveals everything about their CI/CD, testing, and deployment pain points.)
*   **"What is the most painful part of your current tech stack?"** (Every team has pain points. Honest engineers will tell you. If they say "everything is perfect," they are lying.)
*   **"When was the last time the production system went down, and how did the team handle it?"** (Look for a blameless post-mortem culture, not finger-pointing.)

## Red Flags (What NOT to ask)
*   **"What does your company do?"** You should already know this from basic research.
*   **"When will I get a raise/promotion?"** Asking this during the interview makes you seem focused only on money, not the work.
*   **Asking NO questions.** Saying "I don't have any questions" is the biggest red flag. It shows a complete lack of interest in the role.
*   **Asking highly personal questions.** Stick to professional topics.
