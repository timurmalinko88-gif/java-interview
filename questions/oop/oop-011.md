What is the difference between method overriding and method hiding in Java? How do they behave when accessed via a parent class reference? Provide an example.

---ANSWER---

1. **Instance vs. Static:** Overriding applies to instance methods; hiding applies to `static` methods.
2. **Resolution Time:** Overriding is resolved at runtime (dynamic binding). Hiding is resolved at compile time (static binding).
3. **Reference Type:** Hidden methods execute based on the *reference type*. Overridden methods execute based on the *actual object type*.

### Life Analogy
Think of **Method Overriding** like a person's fingerprint: no matter what uniform they wear (the reference type), their fingerprint remains uniquely theirs (the actual object instance).
Think of **Method Hiding** (static methods) like a company dress code: if you wear a "Manager" badge (the reference type), people expect you to follow the manager dress code, regardless of who you actually are.
